Question

As a person who is with his phone 24/7 all year round, I try to understand more about my daily life by analyzing the data collected by my phone. I am interested in the ways I got access to network, places I visited, and how frequently I used my phone.

Data Completeness

I collected data about three indices: the network my phone was connected to, the places I visited, and screen status of my phone. The data was collected between 1/18/2016 and 2/21/2016, and stored in three different tables using MySQL. The network type table contains 34200 entries. The place table contains 215 entries. The screen status contains 7325 entries. The difference in number is because of different time intervals I set to check for those indices. I set large time interval for checking the places I visited rather than the locations I passed. It includes information such as arrival time and departure time, which is different than the location index. I verified completeness of my data by randomly sampling from the entries and searching for missing values.

Data Coherence

Overall my data is coherent. I verified this by converting the first and the last timestamps to dates, and found they corresponded to the dates I started and ended my AWARE study. Also, I randomly sampled timestamps in the middle, and checked the deviation between those selected timestamps and the timestamps before or after them. They all corresponded to the interval I set for an index. Thus, I would draw the conclusion of my data coherence from a statistical standpoint.

Data Correctness

Taken the precision of GPS into consideration, I'd say all my data is reasonable and easy to interpret in some sense. I grouped some locations together since I knew they were near my destinations. I looked through all the locations I collected to make sure its correctness. The other data looked fine too.

Data Accountability

The data is collected by my iphone with an AWARE software installed. Many well-known institutions (i.e. Carnegie Mellon University, Cornell University, etc) have been using this software. The raw data is private and only open to me.

Things We Found

When we first come to the page, it will show a bar chart of network type as default. What we can tell from this bar chart is that I used wifi much more than mobile network. LCZ-NetWork is my wifi network at home and CMU-SECURE is the one on campus. If we click the radius button of Network Type PIE, it will lead us to a pie chart, from which we can clearly see that I used home wifi in almost half of my time.

You may think I stayed at home most of the time this month, which was not the case. If you clicked the radius button of Places Bar, you can clearly tell I stayed on campus almost all the time. From the corresponding pie chart, we can found that I spent almost 70% of my time in CMU.

Why is there this paradox? The reason is that I will usually turn off wifi on my way to school or back home. However, when I reach campus, I usually go directly to a lecture or whatever work in hand in which case I would be very likely to forget to turn on wifi again. So part of campus wifi access would be counted into mobile network. Each time I go home, I feel relaxed and am very likely to turn on wifi for music or video streaming. This explains why the time for home wifi won't lose.

Apart from campus and home, the other places I usually go are two restaurants I dine for lunches or dinners. I also went to Seven Springs for skiing for a whole day, so it was recorded as twice considering the time interval I set.

From the charts of screen status, we can tell the time my phone was on or off was fifty-fifty. The time it was locked or unlocked was also fifty-fifty. This reveals my style of interacting with my phone. I am not very addicted to the screen. In fact, I face my laptop screen almost all day as a CS student, so I do try to avoid using too much 'technology' in leisure time.

Design Thoughts

In order to help readers to interact with the plots and to read the analysis at the same time, I split the page into the left and right parts. The left part is fixed, and readers can refer to any one of the plots as they are browsing the analysis on the right. Also, I keep the overflow text hidden to avoid a possibly overwhelming layout.
